Jones Day Professor of Commercial Law

Professor Lucian A. Bebchuk

James Barr Ames Professor of Law, Economics, and Finance
Director, Program on Corporate Governance
Harvard Law School

Professor Lucian A. Bebchuk is the James Barr Ames Professor of Law, Economics, and Finance at Harvard Law School, where he also serves as the Director of the Program on Corporate Governance. He is one of the world’s leading scholars in the fields of corporate governance, corporate finance, and law and economics, and his work has shaped academic and policy debates for decades.

Professor Bebchuk’s research has been published extensively in the top academic journals in law, economics, and finance. He is a Research Associate at the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) and a Fellow of the European Corporate Governance Institute (ECGI). He has also served as an editor of the Review of Financial Studies, one of the leading academic journals in finance.

Beyond his scholarly contributions, Professor Bebchuk has been actively engaged in public policy discourse. He has testified before committees of the United States Congress on matters related to corporate governance and executive compensation, and has advised governmental and regulatory bodies on these subjects. His work on shareholder rights, the separation of ownership and control, and the roles of institutional investors has been widely cited by courts, regulators, and policymakers.

Professor Bebchuk is the author of numerous influential books and articles, and his scholarship continues to inform debates about how corporate governance frameworks can best serve shareholders, stakeholders, and the broader public interest.
